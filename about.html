Our story

Language is culture. We teach both.

Learn Spanish with Natives grew from a simple frustration: too many language programmes taught grammar perfectly and left students unable to hold a real conversation. We decided to do something different.

What we believe

We believe language is culture.

You can't truly speak a language without understanding the people who live it — their sense of humour, their history, their way of disagreeing, their warmth. Spanish isn't just a set of rules. It's millions of conversations happening right now, shaped by the places and people behind them.

Every teacher we work with is a native speaker. Every cohort is kept small. Every lesson is live. These aren't compromises or selling points — they're the whole point. We built the programme around the things that actually change how you learn: accountability, human connection, and consistent speaking practice with someone who grew up in the language.

We're not trying to be the biggest Spanish school. We're trying to be the one that actually works.

How we teach

Our approach

Every decision we've made about how to run cohorts comes back to one question: what actually helps people speak?

🌊

Immersion from day one

We speak Spanish in class from the very first session. Not exclusively — we're not cruel — but enough that your brain starts making connections you can't make from a worksheet. Discomfort is where learning lives.

🤝

Small groups, real dynamics

Eight students is a number we've settled on deliberately. It's the size where you can't hide, can't coast, and actually have to engage with the material and with each other. The group becomes part of the learning.

💬

Real conversation, always

Drills have their place, but we build every lesson around speaking: scenarios, debates, storytelling, roleplay. Accuracy matters, but fluency is the goal — and fluency comes from talking, not from perfecting grammar exercises.
