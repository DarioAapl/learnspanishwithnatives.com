How it works

Three steps. Eight weeks. One language you'll actually use.

1
🎯

Join a cohort at your level

We match you to a group of 6–8 students at your exact level — Beginner, Intermediate, or Conversational. Groups stay small deliberately, so every session is personal and everyone gets time to speak.

2
💻

Meet twice a week with a native teacher

Live on Zoom, twice a week, with a native from Spain or Latin America. Your teacher brings the culture, the humour, and the real vocabulary that no app or textbook can teach. Class is always interactive, never a lecture.

3
🗣️

Graduate speaking real Spanish

Eight weeks of consistent, guided practice builds the habit. By the final session, you won't just know Spanish — you'll use it. Most students join the next level up within a few weeks of graduating.

Why we do it this way

A native teacher changes everything.

There's a reason fluent speakers sound different from classroom learners. It's not just accent — it's rhythm, humour, and the thousand small choices that make a sentence sound natural rather than translated.

Our teachers grew up with the language. They know how people actually talk, which phrases date you as a tourist, and what makes locals laugh. That's something no algorithm can replicate, and no textbook was written to teach.

Frequently asked

Your questions, answered

We offer three entry levels: Beginner (A1–A2), Intermediate (B1–B2), and Conversational (C1+). If you're unsure where you fit, send us a message and we'll help you figure it out before you book. Placing yourself at the right level makes a meaningful difference to how much you get from the cohort.
Maximum 8 students. We've found this is the sweet spot: small enough that everyone gets real speaking time every session, large enough to have interesting conversations with different voices. We don't run larger groups. Ever.
Sessions are recorded so you can catch up if something comes up. That said, we'd encourage you to attend live whenever you can — the conversation is where the real learning happens, and watching a recording is not the same as being in the room.
Both. Each cohort listing names the teacher and their background, so you can choose based on the accent and dialect that matters most for your goals. Planning a trip to Mexico? Studying for a role in Madrid? We've got both covered.
Short weekly exercises — about 20 minutes between sessions — help you consolidate what you practiced in class. Nothing that requires a textbook. Just enough to keep the language active in your brain between calls.
Most students join the next level up within a month or two. We also keep all alumni in a shared community, send a monthly conversation challenge, and occasionally run informal social events for ongoing practice. Spanish doesn't have to stop when the cohort ends.
